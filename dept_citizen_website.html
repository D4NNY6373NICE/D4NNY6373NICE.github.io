Department of the Citizen

We're responsible for delivering all government services - from Child Benefit and State Pensions to local services like bin collections and tackling fly tipping.

What we do

The Department of the Citizen is the single point of contact for all government services. We've brought together central government benefits and local authority services under one roof to make it easier for citizens to access everything they need.

Our services range from:

  • Benefits and pensions (Child Benefit, Universal Credit, State Pension)
  • Council services (bin collections, housing, council tax)
  • Environmental issues (fly tipping, noise complaints, planning applications)
  • Social services (adult and children's social care)
  • Public health and safety (food hygiene, licensing, trading standards)
  • Transport and highways (road maintenance, parking, public transport)
Service update: We're rolling out our new integrated service platform that lets you manage your benefits, report local issues, and access council services all in one place. Available from next month.

Our priorities

We're focusing on streamlining services and improving user experience:

  • One account for all your government services and benefits
  • Faster processing of benefit claims and local service requests
  • 24/7 online reporting for issues like missed bins and fly tipping
  • Integrated support - benefits advisors who also handle council queries
! Warning Important update: If you receive benefits, you'll need to update your details on our new system by 30th October. Council service users will be migrated automatically.
